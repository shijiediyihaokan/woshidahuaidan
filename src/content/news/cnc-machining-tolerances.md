## Why Tolerances Matter

Tighter tolerances = higher precision = higher cost. Specifying unnecessarily tight tolerances can double or triple your manufacturing cost without improving part performance.

## Standard Tolerance Grades

### ISO 2768 (General Tolerances)

| Grade | Description | Typical Use |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| f (fine) | ±0.05mm to ±0.2mm | Precision components |
| m (medium) | ±0.1mm to ±0.5mm | General engineering |
| c (coarse) | ±0.2mm to ±2mm | Rough parts, weldments |
| v (very coarse) | ±0.5mm to ±4mm | Non-critical dimensions |

### Geometric Tolerances

- **Flatness**: Surface deviation from ideal plane
- **Parallelism**: Deviation between two parallel surfaces
- **Perpendicularity**: 90° angle accuracy
- **Runout**: Circular deviation during rotation
- **Position**: Location accuracy of features

## Best Practices

1. Only tolerance critical functional dimensions
2. Use general tolerances for non-critical features
3. Consider the manufacturing process capabilities
4. Remember: tighter tolerances require more setups and inspection
5. Discuss tolerance requirements with your manufacturer early
