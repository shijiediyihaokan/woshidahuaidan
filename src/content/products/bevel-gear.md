## Product Features

Bevel gears transmit power between intersecting shafts, typically at 90° angles. Our straight bevel gears provide a cost-effective solution for standard right-angle drives, while spiral bevel gears offer smoother, quieter operation for demanding applications.

Each bevel gear set is precisely matched using Gleason-type tooth geometry, ensuring optimal contact patterns and load distribution. CNC machining with post-heat-treatment grinding guarantees consistent quality.

## Technical Specifications

We manufacture both straight and spiral bevel gears in modules from 1 to 16, with ratios ranging from 1:1 to 6:1. Maximum outer diameter capacity is 1200mm.

Material selection depends on application requirements: 20CrMnTi for general use, 40Cr for medium loads, and 42CrMo for high-stress applications. All gears undergo case hardening followed by precision grinding.

## Applications

Bevel gears are critical components in:
- Automotive differentials and transfer cases
- Right-angle gearboxes and speed reducers
- Marine propulsion and steering systems
- Printing and packaging machinery
- Agricultural equipment and harvesters
- Mining and construction machinery

## FAQ

Q: What's the difference between straight and spiral bevel gears?
A: Straight bevel gears have straight teeth and are simpler to manufacture. Spiral bevel gears have curved teeth for smoother engagement, lower noise, and higher load capacity — ideal for high-speed applications.

Q: Can you produce matched gear sets?
A: Yes. All bevel gears are manufactured and inspected as matched pairs to ensure proper tooth contact and quiet operation.
